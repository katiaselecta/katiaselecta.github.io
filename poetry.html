Nothing Left

There's no beauty left,
It's turned to frost,
You may see it in a partner,
Or lover, lost,
To space and time,
And seas uncrossed.

AN

See a picture; reminisce,
Read the words; no longer missed.

If through the eyes, one sees the soul,
And there you meet a tortured girl,
Walk on, head up and shoulders back,
You shan't return, nor shall you crack.

What's in you now is good and gold,
Something She will never know.

He

He's gone, and so what?
He's here, okay good,
And neither one is any good,
So,
Why do you care?

It's fine, they're learning curves,
Or books, or studies, or journals filled,
And never you get what you want, nor need,
For this is a world of greed.

So move on, head up and shoulders back,
You shall grow on and shall not crack,
What's in you now is good as gold,
Something She should have been told.

So,
Carry on and don't look back,
You'll develop soul and strength and tact,
For years to come and years e'en more,
Until you cannot write any more,
For what you have is more than most,
Use it dear or fear the most,
For why we cannot know for sure,
But it's been wtitten long before.

No man can publish this for you,
It's all in here and only for you,
It's hard to say of what's to come,
But take it between forefinger and thumb,
For this gold nib creates your art,
So you can always express this heart,
Kept upon pages,
Bound between leather.

And there you'll find the lost little girl,
Whom had no-one and never will,
For we are always lonesome,
Until we meet that one,
Or come to an end,
And either way you'll never send,
The words to those who need to know,
'Cause this was always your seed to sow,
And why I write so deeply so,
For no apparency to those?
It's mere darkness, I suppose.

Read Something

Read some books,
And hide from the world,
Lord, if I could change it,
I certainly would.

It might be futile,
So I'll dream on,
Take some noxious substances,
And so,
The night will drag on.
